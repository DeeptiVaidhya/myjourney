Screening for colorectal cancer

Colorectal cancer is a cancer that can affect both men and women, and it starts in the colon or rectum. Typically colorectal cancers start as a growth, or polyp, on the inner lining of the colon or rectum. Some polyps can change into cancer over time, but not all will become cancer. These are the American Cancer Society recommendations for colorectal cancer screening:

Age Test Frequency
Men and women age 50 years and older, for all tests gFOBT or FIT test Once per year
Multitarget stoll DNA test Every 3 years
FSIG Every 5 years
Double-contrast barium enema Every 5 years
CT colonography Every 5 years
Colonoscopy Every 10 years
