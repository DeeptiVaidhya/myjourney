Sun protection

Protecting yourself from the sun is a great way to lower your risk of developing a skin cancer. There are lots of ways to practice sun safety when you’re outside.

  • Stay in the shade as much as possible. Direct sunlight can increase your risk for skin cancers.
  • Wear long sleeved shirts and pants to protect your arms and legs from the sun.
  • Wear sunscreen on parts of your body that are uncovered, such as your face, ears, neck, and hands.
  • Wear a hat to create shade for your face. Some hats can also protect the back of your neck from the sun.
  • Use sunglasses to protect your eyes and the sensitive skin around them.
