Screening for cervical cancer

Another common cancer among women is cervical cancer. Cervical cancer is starts in the cells that line the lower part of the uterus, called the cervix. The cervix is the part of the uterus that connects to the vagina. Cancer develops when normal cells in the cervix gradually develop pre-cancerous changes that turn into cancer. Doctors use names such as dysplasia to describe pre-cancerous cells in the cervix. These pre-cancerous cells can be detected with a Pap test and treated to prevent cancer from developing. These are the American Cancer Society recommendations for cervical cancer screening:

Women age 21-29 Pap test Every 3 years
