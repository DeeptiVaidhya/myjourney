Screening for lung cancer

Lung cancer is another cancer than can affect both men and women. Just like it sounds, lung cancer starts in the lungs. There are a few types of lung cancer, but the most common lung cancer is non-small cell lung cancer. Non-small cell lung cancer is most common in people who currently or formerly smoke cigarettes, but it’s also possible to get non-small cell lung cancer if you’ve never smoked. These are the American Cancer Society recommendations for lung cancer screening:

Age Test Frequency
Current or former smokers aged 55-74 in good health CT scan Once per year
