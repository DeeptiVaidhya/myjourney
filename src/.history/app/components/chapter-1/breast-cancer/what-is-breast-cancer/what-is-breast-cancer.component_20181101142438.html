What is breast cancer?
  • Breast cancer is a bunch of cells that grow out of control in the breast tissue and form a tumor.
  • A tumor is cancer, or malignant, if the cells can grow into the surrounding tissues in the breast (invade) or spread to other areas of the body (metastasize).
  • Breast cancer is the most common cancer among women in the U.S., except for skin cancers. One in eight women will develop breast cancer in her lifetime.
  • The stage of breast cancer refers to the size of the cancer when it is detected and whether the cancer has spread to surrounding tissues and organs. When breast cancer stays in the breast tissue, it is called non-metastatic breast cancer.
