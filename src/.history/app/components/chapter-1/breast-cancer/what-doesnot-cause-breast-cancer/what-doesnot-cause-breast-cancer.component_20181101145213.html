What does NOT cause breast cancer?

There are many myths and misconceptions about what can cause breast cancer. You may have come across information at one time or another that says these things are linked to breast cancer. However, research has shown that the following factors are NOT linked to breast cancer.

Credit: NCT

  • Antiperspirants: There is a myth that the chemicals in underarm antiperspirants can cause breast cancer, but this is simply not true.
  • Older women are at greater risk. Most breast cancers are found in women who are 55 years old and older.
  • Another factor is family history. If your mother or sister has had the disease, your chances of developing breast cancer are two times greater.
  • Race and ethnicity are other factors that affect your risk. Overall, white women are slightly more likely to develop breast cancer than African American/black women. However, for women under age 45, breast cancer is more common in African American/black women. Asian, Hispanic, and Native American women have a lower risk of developing breast cancer.
  • Having dense breast tissue also increases your risk of breast cancer. Breasts are made up of fatty tissue, fibrous tissue, and glandular tissue. A woman is said to have dense breasts on a mammogram when they have less fatty tissue and more fibrous and glandular tissue. Women with dense breasts have twice the risk of developing breast cancer than women with non-dense breasts. Unfortunately, dense breast tissue can also make it harder to see cancers on mammograms.
  • Breast cancer may also be related to lifestyle factors. Drinking alcohol, being overweight or obese, and not being physically active are all linked to an increased risk of breast cancer.
