What does NOT cause breast cancer?

There are many myths and misconceptions about what can cause breast cancer.

You may have come across information at one time or another that says these things are linked to breast cancer. However, research has shown that the following factors are NOT linked to breast cancer.

  • Antiperspirants: There is a myth that the chemicals in underarm antiperspirants can cause breast cancer, but this is simply not true.
  • Bras: There is no scientific evidence that bras can cause breast cancer. A 2014 study that included more than 1,500 women found no association between wearing a bra and risk for breast cancer.
  • Induced abortion: Several scientific studies have found that breast cancer risk is unrelated to induced abortions and spontaneous abortions (miscarriages).
  • Breast implants: Breast implants are unrelated to risk of breast cancer. Implants made of silicone can cause scar tissue to form in the breast, and they can make it harder to see breast tissue on mammograms. However, there are other ways to examine breast tissue more completely (such as x-ray pictures called implant displacement views) .
