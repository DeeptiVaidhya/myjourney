Types of treatment for breast cancer

Breast cancer treatments can be local or systemic.

Some breast cancer treatments are local, meaning they treat the tumor without affecting the rest of the body. Other treatments are systemic, because they can reach cancer cells almost anywhere in the body.

Local Treatments Systemic Treatments
Surgery to remove breast cancer and/or nearby lymph nodes Chemotherapy with cancer-killing drugs that may be injected into your vein or taken by mouth
Radiation with high-energy rays or particles that destroy cancer cells Hormone/Endocrine therapy to lower estrogen levels of stop estrogen from acting on breast cancer cells
Targeted therapy with drugs that are designed to block the growth and spread of cancer cells by targeting specific cell changes

Credit: dailycaring.com
