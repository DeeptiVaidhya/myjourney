Screening for skin cancer

Skin cancer is the most common type of cancer, and it can affect both men and women.

There are many different types of skin cancers, and some of the most common are called basal cell or squamous cell skin cancers. These types of skin cancers are most often found in areas that are exposed to the sun. For example, they are commonly found on the head, neck, and arms.

The American Cancer Society does not have specific guidelines for screening for skin cancers. However, many doctors recommend doing self-exams about once per month. It’s important for you to know your own skin well.

The best time to do a skin self-exam is after a bath or shower, in a well-lit room, and in front of a full-length mirror. You can use a hand-held mirror to see areas that are hard to see. You could also have a spouse, close friend, or family member help you with hard to see areas.

The first time you do a self-exam, take some time to carefully go you’re your entire body. Learn your patterns of moles, blemishes, freckles, and other marks on your skin so that you will notice if anything changes.
