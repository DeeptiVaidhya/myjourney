Second cancers after breast cancer

If you have had breast cancer, it’s possible to still get other cancers.

Most women won’t get cancer again, and there are things you can do to help prevent a second cancer. Second cancers are different from cancer recurrence. A cancer recurrence is when your specific cancer comes back after treatment. A second cancer is when a cancer survivor develops a new, unrelated cancer later.

Screening for cancers increases the likelihood of detecting a cancer early. Early detection can often make a cancer easier to treat. The American Cancer Society has formal recommendations for how often you should be screened for different cancers, and how you should be screened. These recommendations can sometimes be different depending on a person’s age.
